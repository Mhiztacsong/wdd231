Find a Therapist

Discover professional mental health support that suits your needs.

Overview of Therapy

Therapy is a process where a licensed mental health professional helps individuals explore their thoughts, feelings, and behaviors to cope with challenges, develop healthy habits, and improve overall well-being.

Type of Therapist

Therapists come from different professional backgrounds. Some of the most common types include:

  • Clinical Psychologist (Ph.D. or Psy.D.): Trained to diagnose and treat mental health disorders using therapy.
  • Licensed Clinical Social Workers (LCSW): Offer general mental health counseling and emotional support.
  • Psychiatrists (M.D. or D.O.): Medical doctors who can prescribe medication and also provide therapy.
  • Marriage and Family Therapists (MFT): Specialize in relationship and family issues.

Why Therapy is Important

Therapy provides you with professional guidance and support for mental health challenges such as anxiety, depression, trauma, and relationship issues. A licensed therapist can help you navigate these difficulties and improve your overall well-being.

Finding the Right Therapist

  • Identify Your Needs: Consider what mental health challenges you're facing and the type of therapy that might help.
  • Online vs. In-Person Therapy: Consider whether you prefer in-person therapy sessions or the convenience of online therapy platforms.

What to Consider When Choosing a Therapist

When choosing a therapist, consider their:

  • Credentials and license
  • Therapeutic approach (e.g., CBT, mindfulness)
  • Experience with your specific issues
  • Comfort and fit after your first consultation

Insurance and Financial Assistance

Check if your therapist accepts insurance, and inquire about sliding scale fees if you're concerned about affordability. Many community centers and non-profits offer low-cost counseling services.

What to Expect in Your First Session

Your first session will typically include an introduction, discussion of confidentiality, and an assessment of your goals. It's an opportunity to ensure the therapist is a good fit for your needs.

Important Questions to Ask

  • What is your experience working with people dealing with [issue]?
  • What is your therapeutic approach?
  • Do you accept insurance or offer sliding scale fees?
