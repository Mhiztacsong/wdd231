Crisis Support Resources

Immediate help and support are available. Please reach out if you need assistance.

Emergency Hotlines

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: Call 1-800-273-TALK (8255) for free, confidential support available 24/7.

Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 to connect with a trained crisis counselor 24/7.

Local Emergency Numbers: Contact your regional or local emergency services if you are in immediate danger. In the U.S., dial 911.

Crisis Text Lines

Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 to connect with a crisis counselor. This free service is available 24/7 for those in emotional distress.

Other Text Lines: Some countries have specific crisis text services. Please check your local resources for more options.

Mental Health Support Apps

Here are some apps that provide accessible mental health support:

  • BetterHelp: Online counseling and therapy with licensed professionals.
  • TalkSpace: Access therapy with a licensed therapist through text, video, or phone sessions.
  • Crisis Text Line: Available via text, providing immediate mental health support.

Find a Crisis Center

For in-person crisis support, you can find a nearby crisis center using the following resource: Find a Crisis Center Near You

Many crisis centers offer walk-in services where individuals can receive support, counseling, and immediate help.

Online Chat Support

If you prefer online communication, you can access live chat support through various services:

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline Chat: Visit their online chat platform for confidential, real-time support.

Advice for Immediate Action

  • Stay Connected: Reach out to a trusted friend or family member for emotional support. Sometimes, just talking to someone can help ground you.
  • Focus on Breathing: Practice deep breathing exercises to calm your mind and body. Try inhaling for 4 seconds, holding for 4 seconds, and exhaling for 4 seconds.
  • Remove Immediate Dangers: If you're feeling overwhelmed, move to a safe place, and ask someone to stay with you until you're feeling more stable.
  • Visit the Emergency Room: If you feel you're in immediate danger or unable to keep yourself safe, go to your nearest hospital emergency room or contact emergency services.
